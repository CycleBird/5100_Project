Project Description
Provide a description of your main project idea. This paragraph should contain the answers to the following questions:
What is the main problem?
	The solution space of many reinforcement learning problems is prohibitively large to 	be exhaustively explored, with the majority of choices being trivial or irrelevant and 	thus wasting computational resources. In this work, we aim to develop a method that 	effectively reduces the solution space, improving training efficiency while preserving 	the model’s expressive capability.
What is the motivation for the project? Why is solving this problem important?
Gomoku is a classical game but the combination exploration process can still be complicated. Efficient self-play mechanism is important to many reinforcement learning problems. Reducing the action space can speed up the training process, require less samples, and make the self-play agent feasible under limited computational resources.
What do you aim to accomplish?
We aim to design a candidate move restriction method to limit the scope that the self-play mechanism considers, and we will integrate this method to the reinforcement learning framework. We aim to show that with our method, the training process converges faster while the performance remains strong. 
What approach do you propose to do what you aim?
We will restrict the candidate move to a smaller set before applying the self-play mechanism, which may include heuristic filtering. We will then train the reinforcement learning agent with limited action space. Finally, we will compare the result with the baseline model with no restrictions applied.
What is the novelty?
Our method will apply an interpretable move restriction heuristics to the existing reinforcement learning framework. We aim to show that our method is able to approximate complex systems with lower computational resources.
Provide a real-world use for your problem, and also describe a toy problem case for your problem that you can start working with.
The real-world use will be AI in board games in general. Ultimately, this general idea can be applied to robotics since we are reducing the action space effectively, requiring less computational resources. The toy problem will be a small Gomoku board, like 6x6 or 8x8. With our method, we will restrict moves to a small set of cells.

[2] K. Fujita, “AlphaViT: A flexible game-playing AI for multiple games and variable board sizes,” PeerJ Computer Science, vol. 11, p. e3403, 2025. Available: https://doi.org/10.7717/peerj-cs.3403 
Existing work has already shown that AlphaZero can be used to learn Gomoku, and some studies have also examined how performance changes across different board sizes. Our project differs from these works because we are not mainly asking whether AlphaZero works for Gomoku or how board size affects the results. Instead, we study whether candidate move restriction can reduce the effective solution space and improve the training efficiency of self-play reinforcement learning.
